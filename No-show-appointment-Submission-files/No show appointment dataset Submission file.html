Introduction¶

In this analysis we'll be looking at the no show appointment dataset to try and predict what factors can help us predict if patients will show up for their scheduled appointments or not. This dataset contains information from medical appointments from Brazil and characteristics about the patients. I go on to analyze and see what characteristics we can use to predict patients showing up for an appointment or not. The variables in this dataset are; PatientId - unique identifier for patients AppointmentID - unique identifier for appointments Gender - sex of the patient(F is Female, M is Male) ScheduledDay - date scheduled for the appointment AppointmentDay - date that the appointment happened Age - is the age of the patient Neighbourhood - where the patient stays Scholarship - whether the patient is a beneficial of the govt fund for under priviledged groups Hipertension - 1 means patient has this illness 0 is they don't Diabetes - 1 means patient has this illness 0 is they don't Alcoholism 1 means patient has this illness 0 is they don't Handcap - 1 means patient has this illness 0 is they don't SMS_received - 1 of the patient received an sms 0 if they didn't No-show - No means the patient showed up for the appointment and yes means they didn't show up

The questions posed for this dataset are; What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

The variables I used for this analysis are ; Dependent variable:No-show | Independent variables were: Gender,Scholarship,Sms received,Hipertension,Diabetes

Conclusions¶

Finding 1.There was some evidence that patients with illnesses like Hipertension or Diabetes were slightly more likely to show up for their appointments compared to those without illness. And from the variables sampled, these two were the only indicators that could be used to try and make this prediction 2.The gender variable performs the same since both Male and female patients had an equal probability of showing up. 3.There were more patients that showed up but didn't receive an sms compared to the ones that received an sms. 83% of those who didn't receive an sms showed up compared to 72% of patients who received an sms and showed up.

Conclusion From the dataset provided and the variables selected, the variables don't strongly indicate a relationship between the chosen variables and a patient showing up or not.

Limitation Simple statistical analysis was carried out to understand the correlation between the chosen variables, no machine learning model were used. This may have limited the results that we could draw from this dataset, compared to when a more statistical approach had been chosen.
